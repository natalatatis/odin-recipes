Tamales Recipe

Back to Home

Description

The best guatemalan tamales out there.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs of corn masa
  • 1 lb of pork meat
  • 10 dried guajillo chilies
  • 5 dried ancho chilies
  • 1 onion
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp oregano
  • Salt to taste
  • Banana leaves or corn husks for wrapping

Steps

  1. Soak the dried chilies in hot water for about 20 minutes until they soften. Remove seeds and stems.
  2. In a blender, combine the soaked chilies, onion, garlic, cumin, oregano, and salt. Blend until smooth to create the sauce.
  3. In a large pot, cook the pork meat until tender. Shred the meat and mix it with the chili sauce. Simmer for 15 minutes to let the flavors meld.
  4. In a separate bowl, prepare the corn masa by mixing it with water and a pinch of salt until it reaches a dough-like consistency.
  5. If using banana leaves, soften them by briefly heating over an open flame or boiling water. If using corn husks, soak them in warm water until pliable.
  6. To assemble the tamales, spread a thin layer of masa onto the center of a banana leaf or corn husk. Add a spoonful of the meat filling on top of the masa. Fold the sides of the leaf or husk over the filling and then fold the bottom up to secure it.
  7. Place the assembled tamales upright in a steamer basket. Steam for about 1 to 1.5 hours, or until the masa is firm and cooked through.
  8. Remove tamales from the steamer and let them cool slightly before unwrapping and serving. Enjoy!
